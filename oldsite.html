About Bert

Bert is a graduate student at Columbia University starting work on his PhD degree in Computer Science. He was born the summer of 1982 in Ithaca, NY. Shortly after, his family moved to the Boston area and he then spent the majority of his childhood growing up in Wellesley, MA. He spent his undergraduate years in Waltham, MA earning a BS and a BA in Computer Science and Philosophy, respectively, at Brandeis University. He then moved to New York City to study at Columbia, where he earned a MS degree in February 2006.

Academics and Research

(Bert's Outdated CV in PDF format.)
Bert's interests lie strongly in the field of Machine Learning. He is also curious about Machine Learning's neighboring fields such as Computational Learning Theory, Computer Vision, and Artificial Intelligence.

While at Brandeis University, Bert took a handful of advanced courses having to do with his current research interests, including an independent study in Machine Learning, Topics in Machine Learning, Philosophy of Cognitive Science, and Computational Neuroscience.

At Columbia, Bert has taken Digital Signal Processing, Computer Vision, Computational Learning Theory, Advanced Computational Learning Theory, Machine Learning, Advanced Machine Learning, Analysis of Algorithms, and worked in the Machine Learning Laboratory run by Professor Tony Jebara. He currently continues his work with the Machine Learning Lab as well as research at the Center for Computational Learning Systems (CCLS).

While not entirely academic, Bert also worked as a part time programmer of analysis tools for the Columbia University Medical Center Neurological Institute's Motor Control Physiology Lab, run by Seth Pullman MD, which does mathematical data analysis of patients' clinical tests.
