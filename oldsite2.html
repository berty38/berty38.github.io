About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at Columbia University working towards his degree in Computer Science. I was born the summer of 1982 in Ithaca, NY. Shortly after, my family moved to the Boston area and I then spent the majority of my childhood growing up in Wellesley, MA. I spent my undergraduate years in Waltham, MA earning a BS and a BA in Computer Science and Philosophy, respectively, at Brandeis University. I then moved to New York City to study at Columbia, where I earned a MS degree in February 2006.

Research

I am a Ph.D. candidate studying machine learning. My advisors are Professor Tony Jebara, director of the Machine Learning Laboratory and Ansaf Salleb-Aouissi, research scientist at the Center for Computational Learning Systems.
